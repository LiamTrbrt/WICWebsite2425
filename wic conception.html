Electronics & Weight Distribution

All electronics are securely mounted onto a sheet of polycarbonate at the behind of the robot with easy access to the kill switch and Crcduino. The battery is place on the end of robot base/frame to counter weight the components such as the lead screw lift system which are located at the front.

Drive System

The current drive system shown in the 3d design is our first iteration. Powered by high speed and torque BaneBot motor, it allows the robot to maneuver the field with ease. The BaneBot motors are mounted rigidly onto the base to the robot with sprockets, each sprocket comes with a chain drive directly powering each wheel, giving us a 4 wheel drive. Our final drive system was changed to a gobilda yellow jacket bevel gear mechanism due to easier replacement of the parts and also due to the fact that they are a lot more reliable and compatible with the arduino code.

Screw Lift

Lead screw mechanism, powered by a gobilda yellow jacket motor allows the claw and other components of the pick-up system to translate upward and downward for easy access to game pieces and loading to the launching system.

Launching system

Launching system made up of 2 main parts, the guiding parabole (walls) and a high rpm flywheel powered by a gobilda yellow jacket motor. The launching system funnels game pieces from the claw after pick-up and, one at a time, shoots them to the desired distance at a 45 degree angle thanks to the flywheel to achieve a landing on the center post.

The Claw

The claw mechanism is made up 3 different main parts. The flip system, the stable finger and the moving finger. The claw itself is a rack and pinion mechanism, the stable finger is made up of a circular 3d printed part which holds up to 3 game pieces at a time while the moving finger has the task of extending outward and pushing the gp’s into the stable finger, all powered by a high torque servo motor. The 3d printed claw is designed in a way that it can easily access all dispensers on the playing field and also drop 1 gp at a time into the launching system even if it is holding multiple. The flip system is the midpoint between the claw and the lift mechanism, it is mounted onto the lead screw and attached to the claw which allows it to rotate the claw a full 360 degrees either way allowing it to dispense gp’s into the launching mechanism smoothly.
